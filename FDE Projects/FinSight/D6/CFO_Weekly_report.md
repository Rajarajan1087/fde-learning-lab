## D6 — Weekly Risk Summary | FinSight Capital

### Week Ending: June 24, 2026

Of the 2,000 loan applications processed this week, 110 (5.5%) were flagged 
as High risk with an average credit score of 557 — 107 points below the 
portfolio average of 664 — and an average loan amount of ₹5,77,317 against 
a Low-risk average of ₹4,55,678, indicating that high-risk applicants are 
simultaneously less creditworthy and seeking larger exposures.

Hyderabad branches recorded the highest default rate at 31.3%, followed by 
Mumbai at 29.9% and Kolkata at 29.6%, suggesting these three geographies 
require enhanced underwriting scrutiny and branch-level risk training before 
the next disbursement cycle.

Home loans and Education loans tied as the highest-risk loan purposes with 
24 high-risk applications each, followed by Vehicle loans at 22 — the credit 
committee should consider tightening minimum credit score thresholds for these 
three categories from the current floor to 620 before approving new 
disbursements in Hyderabad and Mumbai branches.
