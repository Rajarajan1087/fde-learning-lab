Pantaloons Employee Leave Policy
1. Purpose
The purpose of this Leave Policy is to provide a clear and fair framework for employee leave and time off from work. Employees may need time away from work for rest, personal commitments, illness, family responsibilities, emergencies, or special life events. This policy defines the types of leave available, eligibility requirements, approval processes, employee responsibilities, manager responsibilities, and consequences of misuse.
Pantaloons recognizes that employees perform best when they maintain a healthy balance between work and personal responsibilities. At the same time, every employee is expected to ensure that leave usage does not negatively affect store operations, customer service, inventory management, business continuity, or team productivity.
This policy seeks to balance employee welfare with operational requirements.
________________


2. Scope
This policy applies to:
* Corporate office employees
* Retail store employees
* Warehouse and logistics employees
* Regional and zonal office employees
* Full-time employees
* Part-time employees (where applicable)
* Employees on probation
* Fixed-term employees
Temporary workers, consultants, interns, and third-party staff may be governed by separate contractual arrangements.
Where local labor laws provide greater benefits than this policy, the applicable law shall prevail.
________________


3. General Principles
The following principles apply to all forms of leave:
3.1 Leave Is Not an Automatic Right
Employees are entitled to leave as per policy. However, approval depends on business requirements, staffing levels, seasonal demand, and operational needs.
3.2 Advance Notice
Employees should apply for leave as early as possible. Last-minute requests may be denied unless they relate to emergencies or medical situations.
3.3 Accurate Information
Employees must provide accurate reasons for leave requests when required.
Providing false information may result in disciplinary action.
3.4 No Work During Leave
Employees on approved leave are not expected to perform regular work duties unless specifically requested and approved by management.
3.5 Attendance Responsibility
Regular attendance remains a core responsibility of every employee.
Repeated absenteeism, excessive leave usage, or poor attendance patterns may trigger review by management.
________________


4. Annual Leave / Privilege Leave (PL)
Annual Leave is provided to allow employees adequate rest and personal time.
Entitlement
Full-time employees are entitled to:
24 days of Privilege Leave per calendar year
Leave accrues monthly and becomes available proportionately during the year.
Example:
* 2 days earned per month
* Employee joining midway through the year receives prorated entitlement
Usage
Privilege Leave may be used for:
* Vacations
* Personal commitments
* Family events
* Religious activities
* Personal rest and recovery
* Travel
Approval Requirements
Employees should submit requests:
* Minimum 7 days in advance for leave up to 3 days
* Minimum 15 days in advance for leave exceeding 3 days
* Minimum 30 days in advance for leave exceeding 10 consecutive days
Restrictions
Management may decline leave requests during:
* Major sales events
* Festive seasons
* Inventory audits
* Annual stock-taking periods
* Store launches
* Critical operational periods
Carry Forward
Employees may carry forward up to:
15 unused Privilege Leave days
Any balance above 15 days will lapse unless local law requires otherwise.
Encashment
Employees may encash unused leave upon separation where legally required.
________________


5. Casual Leave (CL)
Casual Leave is intended for short-duration personal requirements.
Entitlement
Employees receive:
12 Casual Leave days per year
Appropriate Usage
Examples include:
* Personal errands
* Family functions
* Household emergencies
* Government documentation appointments
* Child-related commitments
* Unexpected personal situations
Conditions
* Normally limited to 3 consecutive days at a time
* Advance notice required whenever possible
* Manager approval required
Carry Forward
Casual Leave cannot be carried forward.
Unused Casual Leave expires at year-end.
________________


6. Sick Leave (SL)
Sick Leave allows employees to recover from illness or injury.
Entitlement
Employees receive:
12 Sick Leave days per year
Usage
Applicable for:
* Fever
* Viral infections
* Medical treatment
* Injury recovery
* Doctor-directed rest
* Mental health conditions requiring rest
Notification Requirement
Employees must notify:
* Reporting manager
* HR (if applicable)
as soon as reasonably possible.
Preferably before the start of the work shift.
Medical Certificate Requirement
Required when:
* Sick leave exceeds 2 consecutive working days
* Pattern of repeated absences is observed
* Management reasonably requests documentation
The company reserves the right to verify medical documentation.
Misuse
Fake medical certificates or false illness claims may result in disciplinary action.
________________


7. Maternity Leave
Pantaloons supports employees during pregnancy and childbirth.
Eligible employees shall receive maternity benefits according to applicable law.
General Guidelines
Maternity Leave may be used:
* Before childbirth
* After childbirth
* During pregnancy-related medical conditions
Documentation
Employees must provide:
* Medical certificate
* Expected delivery date
* Relevant supporting documents
Job Protection
No employee shall be terminated solely due to lawful maternity leave.
________________


8. Paternity Leave
To support employees during childbirth and early childcare responsibilities.
Entitlement
Eligible employees receive:
10 working days of paid Paternity Leave
Usage Period
Must be used:
* Within six months of childbirth
* In one or multiple blocks with approval
Documentation
Birth certificate or hospital records may be requested.
________________


9. Adoption Leave
Employees legally adopting a child may be eligible for:
15 working days of paid leave
Supporting legal documentation must be provided.
________________


10. Bereavement Leave
Employees may need time following the death of a family member.
Entitlement
Up to:
7 calendar days of paid leave
for immediate family members.
Immediate family includes:
* Spouse
* Child
* Parent
* Sibling
* Grandparent
Additional leave may be approved under special circumstances.
________________


11. Marriage Leave
Employees may take leave for their own marriage.
Entitlement
5 working days of paid leave
Available once during employment for the employee's own marriage.
Marriage certificate may be requested.
________________


12. Emergency Leave
Emergency Leave addresses unforeseen situations requiring immediate absence.
Examples include:
* Accidents
* Natural disasters
* Serious family emergencies
* Urgent legal matters
* Medical emergencies involving dependents
Approval may be granted retrospectively after review.
Employees must inform management at the earliest opportunity.
________________


13. Leave Without Pay (LWP)
When all paid leave balances are exhausted, employees may request unpaid leave.
Approval
Approval is not guaranteed.
Management will consider:
* Business needs
* Employee performance
* Attendance history
* Operational impact
Impact
Leave Without Pay may affect:
* Salary
* Incentives
* Attendance bonuses
* Benefit calculations where legally permissible
________________


14. Public Holidays
Pantaloons will observe public holidays according to applicable state and national requirements.
Because retail operations often require staffing during holidays:
* Certain employees may be scheduled to work
* Alternative compensatory off may be provided
* Additional compensation may apply where required by law
________________


15. Compensatory Off (Comp-Off)
Employees required to work on approved holidays or designated off days may receive compensatory leave.
Conditions
* Prior manager authorization required
* Must be recorded officially
* Generally used within 90 days
Unused compensatory leave may lapse.
________________


16. Probationary Employees
Employees on probation are eligible for leave.
However:
* Excessive leave may extend probation periods
* Frequent absences may affect performance evaluation
* Certain leave categories may accrue proportionately
Approval standards may be stricter during probation.
________________


17. Part-Time Employees
Part-time employee leave entitlement shall be calculated proportionately based on working schedules and applicable legal requirements.
________________


18. Store Operations and Blackout Periods
Retail operations experience critical periods where staffing requirements increase significantly.
Examples include:
* Diwali
* Durga Puja
* Christmas
* New Year sales
* End-of-season sales
* Store opening events
* Annual stock audits
During these periods:
* Leave approvals may be restricted
* Long vacations may not be approved
* Priority may be given to emergencies and medical situations
Employees are expected to cooperate with operational requirements.
________________


19. Leave Approval Process
Employee Responsibilities
Employees must:
* Submit requests through approved systems
* Provide accurate information
* Ensure work handover
* Inform relevant stakeholders
* Maintain communication when necessary
Manager Responsibilities
Managers must:
* Review requests fairly
* Avoid favoritism
* Balance business needs with employee welfare
* Respond within a reasonable timeframe
* Document approval decisions
HR Responsibilities
HR shall:
* Maintain leave records
* Monitor compliance
* Resolve disputes
* Conduct audits when required
________________


20. Unplanned Absence Procedure
When employees cannot attend work unexpectedly:
Step 1
Notify manager immediately.
Step 2
Provide expected duration.
Step 3
Submit documentation if required.
Step 4
Maintain communication during extended absence.
Failure to follow these steps may result in unauthorized absence classification.
________________


21. Unauthorized Absence
An absence becomes unauthorized when:
* Employee fails to report to work
* No leave request is submitted
* No manager notification occurs
* Supporting documents are intentionally withheld
* Leave extension is taken without approval
Unauthorized absence may result in:
* Salary deduction
* Written warning
* Performance review
* Disciplinary proceedings
* Termination in severe or repeated cases
________________


22. Abandonment of Employment
Employees absent without authorization for a prolonged period may be treated as having abandoned employment.
Before such action:
* Reasonable contact attempts will be made
* Written communication will be issued
* Opportunity for explanation will be provided
Failure to respond may result in separation from employment.
________________


23. Leave Misuse and Unfair Practices
The following practices are prohibited:
Examples of Misuse
* Providing false medical certificates
* Misrepresenting reasons for leave
* Working elsewhere during paid leave
* Extending leave without approval
* Attendance manipulation
* Coordinated absenteeism
* Repeated absence before or after weekends without justification
* Using sick leave for vacations
* Influencing others to misuse leave
Investigation
The company may investigate suspected misuse.
Methods may include:
* Attendance reviews
* Document verification
* Employee interviews
* Manager reports
Consequences
Depending on severity:
* Verbal warning
* Written warning
* Final warning
* Suspension
* Termination
________________


24. Attendance Expectations
While leave benefits exist, employees are expected to maintain dependable attendance.
Attendance may be reviewed based on:
* Frequency of absences
* Pattern of leave usage
* Business disruption caused
* Compliance with reporting requirements
Consistent attendance may be considered during:
* Promotions
* Transfers
* Performance evaluations
* Incentive decisions
________________


25. Return-to-Work Requirements
Employees returning from extended leave may be required to:
* Meet their manager
* Submit medical fitness documentation
* Complete handover discussions
* Attend reintegration meetings
This helps ensure a smooth return to work.
________________


26. Special Circumstances
Pantaloons reserves the right to provide additional leave support in exceptional circumstances, including:
* Natural disasters
* Public health emergencies
* Civil disruptions
* Serious humanitarian situations
* Government-mandated leave programs
Any such support shall be communicated separately.
________________


27. Policy Interpretation
HR shall be responsible for interpreting this policy.
In situations not specifically covered:
* Applicable law
* Company interests
* Fairness principles
* Operational requirements
shall guide decision-making.

29. Final Employee Responsibility Statement
Every employee of Pantaloons is expected to use leave responsibly and honestly. Leave benefits exist to support employee well-being, family responsibilities, and legitimate personal needs. Abuse of leave impacts customers, colleagues, store operations, and overall business performance.
Employees should plan leave in advance whenever possible, communicate openly with managers, comply with documentation requirements, and return to work as scheduled. Managers are expected to administer this policy consistently and fairly across all teams.
This policy aims to create a workplace where employees can take necessary time off while maintaining the high standards of service, reliability, and operational excellence expected across all Pantaloons locations.
